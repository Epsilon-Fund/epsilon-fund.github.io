## Dominant Narrative
Geopolitical tensions surrounding the US-Iran conflict dictated crypto price action, causing significant volatility but ultimately leading to a recovery on de-escalation news. Concurrently, institutional interest in tokenization and AI-agent payments accelerated, even as the DeFi sector grappled with another major stablecoin exploit and the winding down of Balancer Labs.

## Key Signals
-   **Institutional Accumulation:** Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) expanded capital-raising plans to $44.1B for BTC purchases, adding 1,031 BTC last week. Bitmine Immersion Technologies increased ETH holdings to 4.6M, with ETH rising 18% since the Iran war commenced.
-   **Tokenization Momentum:** BlackRock CEO Larry Fink highlighted tokenized funds as the future, while Nasdaq and Talos partnered to target $35B in "trapped" collateral with an institutional tokenization push.
-   **AI-Agent Payments:** Visa, MoonPay, World Liberty Financial, and Tempo (Stripe) unveiled tools and protocols for AI agents to make on-chain payments, signaling a new frontier for crypto utility.
-   **DeFi Vulnerability:** The Resolv USR stablecoin suffered a $25M exploit due to a compromised key, leading to contagion across DeFi yield vaults. Balancer Labs also shut down its corporate entity after a $110M exploit.
-   **Prediction Market Scrutiny:** A bipartisan Senate bill was introduced to ban sports betting on prediction markets, prompting Polymarket and Kalshi to tighten insider trading rules.

## Sector Watch
The DeFi sector experienced a turbulent week, marked by the corporate shutdown of Balancer Labs following a $110M exploit and a $25M exploit of the Resolv USR stablecoin. The Resolv hack, attributed to a compromised key, caused contagion across yield vaults, exposing persistent structural vulnerabilities. This underscores the fragility of certain DeFi models and the ongoing need for robust security, even as institutional interest in tokenized assets grows elsewhere.

## Regulation & Macro
Geopolitical tensions from the US-Iran conflict drove macro uncertainty, causing market volatility and influencing crypto price action, with a rebound on news of postponed strikes. Regulatory developments included the SEC's proposed crypto interpretation moving to White House review, a bipartisan Senate bill targeting prediction markets, and restrictive language in the Clarity Act regarding stablecoin yield. Delaware is also advancing a stablecoin licensing framework.

## Epsilon's Read
The market's immediate future is still dictated by geopolitical risk, with BTC acting as a high-beta asset rather than a safe haven, as seen in its $60B market cap swing on Iran news. However, institutional conviction remains strong, evidenced by Strategy's $44.1B capital raise for BTC and Bitmine's 18% ETH outperformance since the conflict began. While DeFi continues to battle exploits (Resolv $25M), the accelerating trend of tokenization and AI-agent payments represents a structural tailwind that will decouple long-term crypto growth from short-term macro volatility. We remain bullish on assets facilitating this integration.
