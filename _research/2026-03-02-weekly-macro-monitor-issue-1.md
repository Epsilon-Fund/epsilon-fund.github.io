## Economic Summary

**Labour:** NFP came in at 275k vs 200k expected — strong headline but prior months revised down significantly. Unemployment ticked up to 3.9%. Wage growth moderating at 4.3% YoY.

**Inflation:** CPI printed 3.2% YoY vs 3.1% expected. Core remains sticky at 3.8%. Services inflation the main driver — shelter and transportation costs elevated. Fed in no rush to cut.

**Activity:** ISM Services beat at 52.6 vs 51.8 expected. Manufacturing remains in contraction at 47.8. Consumer spending resilient but showing early signs of fatigue at lower income cohorts.

**China:** PMI contracted for second straight month at 49.1. Property sector stress continues with new home prices falling in 70 of 70 tracked cities. Stimulus measures announced but market reaction muted.

## Markets Performance

**Global Equities:** S&P 500 -1.8%, Nasdaq -2.4% as rate-sensitive tech sold off. STOXX 600 -0.9%, outperforming on ECB cut expectations. Nikkei -1.1% on JPY strength. MSCI EM -2.3% on DXY pressure.

**Fixed Income:** UST curve bear flattened — 2Y +8bp, 10Y +11bp. IG spreads widened modestly to 95bp. HY held in relatively well. Bund yields +6bp following stronger EU data.

**FX:** DXY broke above 104 on hot CPI and strong payrolls. EUR/USD -1.1% to 1.0821. JPY weakening resumed after brief reversal. EM FX broadly weaker with MXN and BRL underperforming.

**Commodities:** WTI -2.1% on demand concerns and stronger dollar. Gold flat — holding $2,300 support despite real yield pressure. Copper -1.4% on China PMI weakness.

## Main Stories

**1. Fed Holds, Signals Patience On Cuts**
The Fed held rates at 5.25-5.50% as expected but Chair Powell's press conference struck a more hawkish tone than markets anticipated. Powell emphasised the need for "greater confidence" on inflation before cutting, pushing back on June cut pricing. Market now prices first cut in September.

**2. China PMI Contracts For Second Month**
China's manufacturing PMI fell to 49.1, missing expectations of 49.9 and marking the second consecutive month of contraction. The data reinforces concerns about the durability of China's recovery and adds to commodity demand headwinds. Government has signalled additional fiscal support but specifics remain vague.

**3. ECB Prepares For June Cut**
ECB officials signalled growing comfort with a June rate cut as Eurozone inflation continues to decline. Core CPI fell to 2.9% in the flash estimate. EUR/USD weakness reflects the widening rate differential with the US as Fed pushes back cut timing.

## Week Ahead

**Key Watch:** US CPI on Wednesday is the single most important print of the week. A second consecutive upside surprise would further delay Fed cut expectations and could push the 10Y above 4.5% — a meaningful headwind for risk assets including crypto.

## Sell-Side Views

**GS:** Raised US GDP tracking to 2.8% for Q1. Still sees two Fed cuts in 2024 but pushed first cut to July. Overweight equities, underweight duration.

**MS:** Global growth moderating but avoiding recession. Prefer EM duration over US. Cautious on US equities at current valuations given rate repricing.

**JPM:** Recession risk at 25%, lower than consensus. Sees Fed cutting in June if CPI cooperates. Overweight credit, selective on equities.

**Consensus shift:** Bloomberg consensus has shifted from pricing 6 cuts at start of year to now pricing 2-3. Significant positioning unwind in rate-sensitive assets still possible if data stays hot.
