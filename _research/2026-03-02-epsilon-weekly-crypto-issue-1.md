**Big Story:** Hot CPI data and a hawkish Fed pushed risk assets lower across the board. Crypto followed, with BTC declining 4.3% before finding support at $65,000. For the first time in six weeks, spot BTC ETFs saw net outflows — $285M left the market, signalling short-term institutional caution.

**Bottom Line:** A risk-off week driven by macro, not crypto-specific factors. The structural bull case remains intact but the market needs CPI to cooperate before resuming the uptrend.

## What The Data Says

**Key On-Chain Signal — Exchange Reserves:**
Exchange reserves rose 2.1% this week — coins moving back onto exchanges, a short-term bearish signal suggesting some holders are preparing to sell. Not alarming at this level but worth monitoring if the trend continues next week.

**Institutional Activity:**
ETF flows turned negative for the first time in six weeks. Net outflows of $285M across all spot BTC ETFs. IBIT saw the largest single-day outflow since launch at $112M on Thursday. Institutional money is cautious but not panicking — this looks like profit-taking rather than a structural exit.

**What This Means:**
Short-term headwinds from macro and institutional caution. However, exchange reserves remain near multi-year lows on a longer timeframe and the halving cycle is structurally supportive. Dips into the $62,000-$65,000 range remain buyable for patient capital.

## Macro Check

**Traditional Markets:** S&P 500 -1.8%, Nasdaq -2.4%. Hot CPI print at 3.2% pushed rate cut expectations further out — market now pricing first Fed cut in September vs June previously. DXY rallied 1.2% to 104.2, a meaningful headwind for dollar-denominated assets.

**Crypto Impact:** The DXY rally and rate repricing were the primary drivers of crypto weakness this week. BTC's correlation with Nasdaq remains elevated at 0.68 on a 30-day basis. Until macro clears — specifically until CPI starts cooperating — crypto will continue trading as a risk asset.

## Altcoins & Trends

**What's Hot:** SOL +2.1% — outperforming on strong DeFi activity and continued NFT volume. The Solana ecosystem narrative remains the dominant alt story heading into Q2. ONDO +8.4% leading the RWA sector which is quietly accumulating interest from institutional participants.

**What's Not:** ETH -5.1%, underperforming BTC this week. ETH/BTC ratio hit a three-month low. Layer 2 tokens broadly weak — ARB -9.2%, OP -7.8%. AI tokens gave back recent gains with FET -12% and RENDER -8%.

## Week Ahead

**Watch For:**
- US CPI Wednesday — the most important macro event of the week. Hot print = more pain for crypto. In-line or soft = potential relief rally
- ETF flow data — watch whether this week's outflows are a one-off or the start of a trend
- BTC $63,000 support — a daily close below this level would be technically significant

**Key Levels:**
- BTC Support: $63,000 / $58,000 | Resistance: $69,000 / $73,000
- ETH Support: $3,000 / $2,800 | Resistance: $3,400 / $3,600

**Our Take:** We are in a consolidation phase driven by macro uncertainty. The structural crypto bull case — halving cycle, ETF adoption, improving regulation — remains intact. However the near-term path depends heavily on inflation data. We are reducing position sizes slightly and waiting for CPI clarity before adding new longs. Patience is the edge right now.
